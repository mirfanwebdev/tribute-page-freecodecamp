J. Robert Oppenheimer

the father of atomic bomb

The timeline of Oppenheimer's life:
  • 1904 - Born in New York
  • 1925 - Earned bachelor's degree in chemistry from Havard University
  • 1927 - Received his doctorate from Gottingen University
  • 1936 - Became full proffessor at University of California. Made contribution of quantum mechanics and nuclear physics such as the Born–Oppenheimer approximation and Oppenheimer–Phillips process in nuclear fusion
  • 1942 - Selected to be head of Manhattan Project, tasked with developing the first nuclear weapon
  • 1945 - Wittnesses the first nuclear bomb test in the New Mexico Desert. The weapon later used in the bombing of Hiroshima and Nagasaki. He is quoted in saying, "I am become Death, the destroyer of worlds."
  • 1947 - Appointed as the Chairman of the General Advisory Committee of the Atomic Energy Commission
  • 1953 - Suspended from the GAC of the AEC for having communist ties. Most of his family and friends were members of the Communist Party.
  • 1963 - Received the Enrico Fermi Award, "for contributions to theoretical physics as a teacher and originator of ideas, and for leadership of the Los Alamos Laboratory and the atomic energy program during critical years."
  • 1967 - dies from throat cancer
